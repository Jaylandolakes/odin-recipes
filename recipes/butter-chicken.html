Butter Chicken


The Velvet Crown Jewel of Indian Cuisine

Butter chicken, or "murgh makhani," is a beloved Indian dish that features tender pieces of chicken simmered in a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce. Itâ€™s typically spiced with a blend of garam masala, ginger, garlic, and sometimes fenugreek, creating a harmonious balance of flavors. The sauce is velvety and slightly sweet, with a mild heat that makes it accessible to a wide range of palates.

The dish originated in the 1950s at the Moti Mahal restaurant in Delhi, where leftover chicken was cooked with butter, cream, and tomatoes to prevent waste and create a flavorful meal. Over the years, it has gained immense popularity both in India and around the world, becoming a staple in Indian cuisine. The combination of spices and creamy texture makes butter chicken a comforting and indulgent dish that pairs perfectly with naan or rice.


Ingredients

  • Free-range chicken (preferably organic, high-quality cuts)

  • Greek yogurt (strained, for rich marination)

  • Freshly squeezed lemon juice (from organic lemons)

  • Young ginger (fresh, for vibrant flavor)

  • Black garlic (for depth and sweetness)

  • Homemade garam masala (using high-quality whole spices)

  • Fresh turmeric root (for a brighter flavor)

  • Smoked paprika (for a subtle smokiness)

  • Cumin seeds (to toast and grind fresh)

  • Coriander seeds (to toast and grind fresh)

  • Maldon sea salt (or high-quality sea salt)

  • European-style cultured butter (for a rich, tangy flavor)

  • Heavy whipping cream (preferably organic, high-fat content)

  • San Marzano tomatoes (for exceptional flavor)

  • Shallots (for a sweeter, more refined onion flavor)

  • Thai green chilies (for heat and complexity)

  • Micro cilantro (for garnish and fresh flavor)

  • Cold-pressed grapeseed oil (or high-quality ghee)

  • Raw cane sugar (to balance flavors)

  • Dried kasuri methi (carefully sourced for quality)

  • Fresh bay leaves (for enhanced aroma)

  • Green cardamom pods (freshly sourced)

  • Ceylon cinnamon stick (for a unique sweetness)

  • Basmati rice (aged, for texture and aroma)

  • Artisan naan (from a high-quality local bakery)
